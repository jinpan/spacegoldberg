The Discovery

“It’s an assist! A gravity assist!” Your head physicist tumbles into the room. Pushing up her glasses, she sets an old, dusty textbook down on the desk in front of you. She flips it open to a page and points excitedly. “See! See! It’s not a miracle, it’s science!” The page is full of equations and diagrams.

“When we travel close to the planet, we are accelerated by the planet’s gravity. This is what causes our abrupt change in direction and increase in speed!” Your head physicist is smiling from ear to ear.

You, on the other hand, are not smiling. An engineer has brought you a diagram of the next step they propose for leaving the solar system. And this one involves going around two planets.
